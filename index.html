About

For me, a big part of yoga has always been about community and family – practicing yoga as unity, not only of mind, body and soul but also off the mat, in the connections we form. Yoga has been in my life since I was very little, when my mum gave me a book, ‘animal yoga for children’, which started me off! I have now been practicing yoga for many years - exploring the beauty of the ashtanga and vinyasa styles. Learning about the power of yoga as a healing tool, and developing a wider understanding of the practice beyond asana, led me to pursue a 200-hour Ashtanga Vinyasa teacher training with John Scott Yoga Senior Teacher, Sandra Howling. I want to share this transformational practice with others, and am dedicated to honouring the ancient roots of yoga, whilst making it accessible and inclusive for everyone.

My classes are strong and dynamic, and there are always different options to make the practice work for each individual. I offer verbal and hands-on adjustments to support students to safely access deeper asana and alignment. Looking forward to meeting you on the mat!

Celeste x
